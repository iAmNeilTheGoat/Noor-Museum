## üìÇ Project Overview

**Noor-Museum** is a high-context, experimental roleplay framework designed to evaluate the creative, logical, and context-retention capabilities of advanced Large Language Models (LLMs). 

The experiment simulates a dialogue between two specialized AI agents curating a "Museum of the Invisible"‚Äîan institution dedicated to preserving intangible artifacts such as lost sounds, forgotten silences, and fleeting emotional states.

### üé≠ The Protocol
Models are instructed to alternate between two distinct personas:
1.  **Agent Alpha (The Acquisitionist):** A field agent responsible for discovering and describing intangible artifacts. Their tone is poetic, mysterious, and narrative-driven.
2.  **Agent Beta (The Critic-Architect):** A curator responsible for designing the physical and metaphysical spaces to house these artifacts. Their tone is academic, structural, and focused on phenomenology.

This repository contains the full conversation logs, analysis reports, and performance metrics for eight distinct AI models.

1.  **Creativity is Not Size-Dependent:** The **MoonShot Kimi** (unknown size) and **Qwen (32B)** models significantly outperformed the **Llama 70B** model in terms of conceptual novelty. Smaller, specialized models can punch above their weight in creative tasks.
